What Is Cold Email Lead Generation?
Cold email lead gen means we find the exact decision-makers who need your services — before they go searching for someone else — and reach out with a targeted message that gets replies.

Most logistics companies grow by referral or word of mouth. That works — until it doesn't. Cold email builds a system that consistently puts Sierra Express in front of property managers, business owners, and procurement contacts across your 10 target industries, on autopilot.

We use professional sending infrastructure (not Gmail or Yahoo), write multi-step email sequences tailored to each audience, and manage the technical setup so your domain stays clean and deliverable. The result: a steady flow of interested prospects reaching out to you each month.

How the Technical Setup Works — Domain Buying & Warm-Up

This is the part most people don't know about — and it's why our campaigns actually land in inboxes instead of spam folders.

Step 1 — We Buy Dedicated Sending Domains

We purchase separate email domains specifically for outreach — for example, sierraexpressllc.com or sierraexpressdelivers.com. These are never your main business domain. This is critical — it protects your real domain's reputation so your day-to-day business email is never affected.

Step 2 — We Set Up Multiple Sending Inboxes

Multiple email accounts are created under these domains (e.g., ryan@sierraexpressllc.com, james@sierraexpressllc.com). Spreading sends across multiple inboxes keeps daily volume per account low — a key signal Google uses to determine whether you're a legitimate sender.

⏱ Step 3 — 14-Day Domain Warm-Up Period

This is the most important part of the process. When a brand-new domain starts sending hundreds of emails right away, Google and other email providers immediately flag it as spam. To prevent this, every new domain must go through a 14-day warm-up period before the real campaign begins.

During warm-up, our software automatically sends small volumes of emails between real inboxes, simulates opens and replies, and gradually builds a positive sender reputation with Google. By Day 14, the domains are trusted — and your campaign emails land in the inbox, not the spam folder.

Step 4 — SPF, DKIM & DMARC Configuration

We configure all the technical email authentication records for each domain. These tell Gmail and Outlook that your emails are legitimate and coming from an authorized sender — without these, emails get flagged immediately regardless of content.

Step 5 — Campaign Goes Live on Day 14

Once warm-up is complete, the full campaign launches. Emails go out on a controlled schedule — typically 40–80 sends per inbox per day — keeping volume safe and deliverability high throughout the entire campaign.
